Research Publications

My research focuses on theoretical and computational aspects of quantum mechanics, cosmology, and gravitational physics. I explore foundational questions in quantum foundations, dark matter models, and black hole thermodynamics, often using numerical simulations and analytical methods. Below are selected publications with links (where available).

2025 March

Quantum Entanglement in Cosmological Spacetimes

Mohammad Yasin, A. Rahman

Physical Review D (under review)

This paper investigates entanglement entropy in expanding universes, proposing a novel measure for quantum correlations across cosmic horizons. We demonstrate that entanglement can serve as a probe for dark energy dynamics.

2024 November

Modified Gravity Models for Galactic Rotation Curves

Mohammad Yasin

Journal of Astrophysics and Astronomy

We propose a scalar-tensor gravity extension that reproduces flat rotation curves without dark matter. The model is tested against SPARC database and shows consistency with observational data at galactic scales.

2024 June

Black Hole Information Paradox Revisited

Mohammad Yasin, S. Karim

Conference Proceedings, International Conference on Quantum Gravity

A semi-classical approach combining Hawking radiation with quantum hair is presented, offering a potential resolution to the information loss problem without invoking firewalls or non-locality.

2023 October

Numerical Simulation of Bose-Einstein Condensates in Rotating Traps

Mohammad Yasin

Journal of Low Temperature Physics

Using Gross-Pitaevskii equation simulations, we study vortex lattice formation in rotating BECs. The results reveal new stable configurations at high angular momentum, with implications for superfluid turbulence.
